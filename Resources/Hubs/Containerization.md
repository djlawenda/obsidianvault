# Containerization

Containerization is a lightweight virtualization technology that packages applications and their dependencies into isolated units called containers, sharing the host operating system. This approach offers benefits like portability, resource isolation, and efficient resource utilization.

*   Containers are a good way to bundle and run your applications.

*   Containers are similar to VMs, but they have relaxed isolation properties to share the Operating System (OS) among the applications. Therefore, containers are considered lightweight.

*   As they are decoupled from the underlying infrastructure, they are portable across clouds and OS distributions.
